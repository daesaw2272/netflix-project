Introduction

The rapid growth of streaming platforms has transformed how audiences consume entertainment content. Services such as Netflix provide thousands of movies and television shows from different countries, genres, and production periods. Understanding the composition and trends within streaming catalogues can provide insights into platform strategies, content diversity, and audience targeting.

This project analyses the Netflix titles dataset to explore patterns in the platform’s content library. Using exploratory data analysis and data visualisation techniques, the study examines the distribution of movies and television shows, trends in content additions over time, genre popularity, production countries, content ratings, and movie duration patterns. The goal of this analysis is to identify key characteristics of Netflix’s catalogue and gain insights into the platform’s content strategy.
